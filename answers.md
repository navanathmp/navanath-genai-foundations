# Question 1

The Bag-of-Words model calculates similarity based on word frequency. Since the word "light" appears in multiple sentences, the model may show similarity even when the meanings are different. This creates a false mathematical relationship because the model does not understand context.

# Question 2

Bag-of-Words loses contextual meaning and word order. It only stores word counts, making it difficult for search engines and chatbots to understand the intended meaning of words. Polysemy and semantic relationships are lost.

# Question 3

Modern Large Language Models use context-aware embeddings and self-attention mechanisms. Self-attention analyzes surrounding words to determine meaning. Therefore, the word "light" receives different vector representations depending on whether it refers to food texture or illumination.
